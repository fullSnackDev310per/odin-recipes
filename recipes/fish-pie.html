Nigerian Fish Pie

Description

Nigerian fish pie is an alternative to the quintessential party snack known as meat pie. However, I must say I prefer the rich umami flavor that a fish pie offers. Unlike the traditional British fish pie, which is typically baked, the Nigerian fish pie is deep-fried dough filled with fish, such as mackerel or sardines, along with various spices and vegetables.

Ingredients

Filling Ingredients

  • 2 Cans of Sardines
  • 2 Tablespoons Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Teaspoons Dried Thyme
  • 1 Medium Onion
  • 3 Large Carrots
  • 3 Large Potatoes
  • 1/2 Fish Stock Cube

Dough Ingredients

  • 3 Cups of All Purpose Flour
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1 Egg
  • 200 Grams Butter
  • 1 Cup of Milk
  • 1 egg for basting

Steps

  1. Mix the salt and flour in a bowl.
  2. Add the butter and egg. Then mix wth your hands and add the milk in increments as you mix. .
  3. Once mixed, divided into two dough balls and store one-half in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  4. Chop and dice the vegetables for filling.
  5. Stir fry with seasoning till fragrant before adding the Sardines.
  6. Leave to simmer till vegetables are cooked. Stir well until fish is properly crushed.
  7. Taste and turn off the heat.
  8. Next, bring out the dough from the fridge and roll out with a rolling pin.
  9. Cut into round shapes (discs) with an empanada mould, meat pie cutter or a small cup or plate or any round object.
  10. Brush with egg white.
  11. Scoop the filling in the cut dough and seal.
  12. Place on a lightly grased baking pan.
  13. Preheat your oven for 20 minutes at 400F (200C) for medium sized pies and 375F (190F) for smaller pies.
  14. Brush your sealed dough with the egg white and place in a pre-heated oven.
  15. Bake till brown. Once brown, that's it. Your crunchy Nigerian fish pie is ready.
